Overview

The Comprehensive Healthcare Support Scheme, widely known as 'Arogya Sahaya', is a flagship initiative of the Government of Andhra Pradesh aimed at providing universal access to quality healthcare for all eligible families. The scheme offers financial protection against catastrophic health expenditures by covering hospitalization and surgical procedures for a wide range of diseases.

This scheme operates through a network of empanelled public and private hospitals, ensuring that beneficiaries can avail cashless treatment. Since its inception, Arogya Sahaya has significantly reduced out-of-pocket expenses for medical treatments and improved health outcomes for millions of citizens in Andhra Pradesh.

Key Features of the Scheme

  • Cashless treatment up to ₹5,00,000 per family per year
  • Coverage for over 2,000 medical procedures and therapies
  • Access to a wide network of empanelled hospitals (public and private)
  • Pre and post-hospitalization expenses covered
  • Free health camps and diagnostic services in rural areas
  • Dedicated helpline and support system for beneficiaries

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the Comprehensive Healthcare Support Scheme (Arogya Sahaya), families must meet the following criteria:

Residency

Must be a resident family of Andhra Pradesh

Income Criteria

Annual family income should be below a specified threshold (e.g., ₹5,00,000 per annum, subject to revision)

Ration Card

Typically, families holding specific types of ration cards (e.g., White Ration Card) are eligible

Exclusion Criteria

Government employees and their families covered under separate health schemes may be excluded

Note: All members of an eligible family are covered under the scheme. Specific criteria and enrollment drives are announced periodically by the government.

Benefits

The Comprehensive Healthcare Support Scheme offers substantial benefits to ensure affordable and quality medical care:

Cashless Hospitalization

Cashless treatment at empanelled hospitals for covered procedures, up to the annual limit

Medicine Coverage

Cost of medicines and diagnostic tests during hospitalization and for a specified post-hospitalization period

Wide Range of Procedures

Coverage for a vast list of surgical and medical treatments including critical illnesses

Transportation Allowance

Allowance for transportation to the hospital in certain cases

Pre & Post Hospitalization

Coverage for medical expenses incurred up to 10 days before admission and up to 30 days after discharge

Maternity & Newborn Care

Comprehensive coverage for maternity-related procedures and newborn care

Required Documents

The following documents are generally required for enrollment and availing benefits under the Healthcare Support Scheme:

Aadhaar Card

For all family members to be enrolled

Ration Card

Valid White Ration Card or other specified ration card indicating eligibility

Income Certificate

May be required if ration card criteria are not solely used for income verification

Doctor's Prescription/Referral

For availing specific treatments or hospitalization at the time of need

Photographs

Passport-sized photographs of family members (may be captured during enrollment)

Note: An Arogya Sahaya Health Card is issued to enrolled families. This card must be presented at empanelled hospitals to avail cashless services.

Application Process (Enrollment & Availing Benefits)

Enrollment is often done through specific drives or at designated centers. Availing benefits at hospitals involves these steps:

1

Enrollment

Eligible families get enrolled during government-conducted drives or through MeeSeva/Ward Sachivalayam centers. An Arogya Sahaya card is issued.

2

Identify Empanelled Hospital

In case of medical need, identify a nearby empanelled hospital. The list is available online and via helpline.

3

Approach Arogyamitra

At the hospital, contact the 'Arogyamitra' (scheme coordinator) with the Arogya Sahaya card and doctor's advice.

4

Pre-authorization

The hospital seeks pre-authorization from the scheme authorities for the proposed treatment.

5

Cashless Treatment

Upon approval, the patient receives cashless treatment for covered procedures.

6

Post-Discharge Support

Follow-up consultations and medicines for a specified period post-discharge are also covered.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I need treatment not available in my district?

The scheme allows for treatment in any empanelled hospital across Andhra Pradesh, and in some cases, in empanelled hospitals in neighboring states for specific super-specialty treatments.

Are all diseases covered under this scheme?

The scheme covers a wide list of over 2,000 procedures and therapies for various diseases. However, some conditions or purely cosmetic procedures might be excluded. The detailed list is available on the scheme portal.

How do I get an Arogya Sahaya Health Card?

Eligible families are enrolled during special drives or can apply through MeeSeva centers or Village/Ward Secretariats. Once enrolled, the health card is issued.

What if an empanelled hospital asks for money?

Empanelled hospitals are supposed to provide cashless treatment for covered procedures. If any hospital demands money, you should immediately report it to the Arogyamitra at the hospital or call the scheme's helpline.

Can I choose any hospital for treatment?

You can choose any hospital from the list of empanelled hospitals under the scheme. It's advisable to check if the required specialty treatment is available at the chosen hospital.

Healthcare Support Scheme Information

Enrollment for the Healthcare Support Scheme (Arogya Sahaya) is typically done through designated enrollment centers (MeeSeva, Village/Ward Secretariats) or during special government drives.

Key Steps for Enrollment:

  • Visit the nearest designated enrollment center.
  • Carry Aadhaar cards for all family members.
  • Carry your family's Ration Card (White Card or other eligible card).
  • Provide income details if required.
  • Biometric information and photographs may be captured.
  • Upon successful enrollment, an Arogya Sahaya Health Card will be issued.

For assistance, please contact the Arogya Sahaya Helpline: 104
