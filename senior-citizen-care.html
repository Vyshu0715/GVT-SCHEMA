Overview

The Senior Citizen Care Scheme, known as 'Aadhaarana', is a comprehensive initiative by the Government of Andhra Pradesh dedicated to the welfare of its elderly population. Recognizing the contributions of senior citizens and their special needs, this scheme aims to provide a safety net encompassing financial assistance, healthcare facilities, and social engagement opportunities.

Aadhaarana integrates various existing and new programs, including old-age pensions, specialized healthcare services, establishment of day-care centers (recreation centers), and support for old age homes. The overarching goal is to ensure that senior citizens lead a healthy, dignified, and active life, free from neglect and insecurity.

Key Features of the Scheme

  • Enhanced monthly old-age pension
  • Specialized geriatric healthcare services at government hospitals
  • Free health check-ups and medicines for common age-related ailments
  • Establishment and support of senior citizen day-care centers and recreation clubs
  • Provision for assistive devices (e.g., walking sticks, hearing aids)
  • Helpline and counseling services for senior citizens
  • Awareness programs on rights and welfare of senior citizens

Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility for the Senior Citizen Care Scheme (Aadhaarana) and its components generally includes:

Age

Must be 60 years of age or above (may be lower for certain categories like widows under pension schemes)

Residency

Must be a permanent resident of Andhra Pradesh

Vulnerability / Income

Often targeted towards destitute, BPL (Below Poverty Line) senior citizens, or those with low family income for financial aid components. Healthcare and social components may have broader eligibility.

Specific Needs

For assistive devices or specialized care, medical assessment may be required.

Note: Eligibility for specific benefits like pensions, healthcare subsidies, or old age home admission will have detailed criteria. The scheme aims to be inclusive, particularly for those without adequate family support.

Benefits

The Senior Citizen Care Scheme offers a multi-faceted support system:

Monthly Pension

Regular financial assistance through the state's old-age pension scheme (e.g., YSR Pension Kanuka)

Healthcare Access

Priority access to geriatric care, free consultations, diagnostics, and medicines at public health facilities

Assistive Devices

Provision of aids like spectacles, hearing aids, wheelchairs, and walking sticks to needy seniors

Day Care Centers

Access to recreation, social activities, and basic health monitoring at senior citizen day-care centers

Helpline & Counseling

Dedicated helpline for assistance, grievance redressal, and emotional support

Support for Old Age Homes

Financial and infrastructural support to government and NGO-run old age homes for destitute seniors

Required Documents

Common documents required for various components of the Senior Citizen Care Scheme:

Age Proof

Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, School Certificate, or Medical Certificate indicating age

Identity & Residence Proof

Aadhaar Card (primary), Ration Card, Voter ID

Income Proof / BPL Status

Income certificate or BPL card for financial assistance components like pension

Bank Account Details

Passbook copy of an active bank account (preferably linked to Aadhaar) for pension disbursement

Photographs

Recent passport-sized photographs

Medical Certificate

For specific health needs or disability claims, if applicable

Note: Applications for pensions are often routed through Village/Ward Secretariats. For other services, contact the local Social Welfare office or designated centers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current old-age pension amount in Andhra Pradesh?

The pension amount under YSR Pension Kanuka is subject to government revisions. Please check the latest official announcements or the YSR Pension Kanuka portal for the current amount.

How can I access specialized geriatric care?

Many government district hospitals and some CHCs/PHCs have dedicated geriatric clinics or services. You can inquire at your nearest public health facility or call the health helpline (104).

Are there any charges for staying in government-supported old age homes?

Services in government-run or fully supported old age homes for destitute seniors are generally free. NGO-run homes may have varying fee structures, some of which might be subsidized under this scheme for eligible individuals.

How can one report neglect or abuse of a senior citizen?

You can contact the Senior Citizen Helpline (often 14567 nationally, or specific state helplines), local police, or the District Social Welfare Officer. The Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007, provides legal recourse.

What kind of activities are available at senior citizen day-care centers?

Day-care centers typically offer recreational activities (indoor games, reading), social interaction, health talks, yoga sessions, and sometimes basic health monitoring or counseling.
