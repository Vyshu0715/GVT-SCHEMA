Overview

The Affordable Housing Scheme, also known as 'Nivas Sahaya', is a pivotal initiative by the Government of Andhra Pradesh to ensure "Housing for All." The scheme aims to provide pucca houses with basic amenities to eligible homeless families from Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) and Low-Income Groups (LIG) in both urban and rural areas of the state.

This scheme involves the construction of new housing units, provision of financial assistance for self-construction on owned land, or credit-linked subsidies for home loans. By addressing the critical need for shelter, Nivas Sahaya contributes to improved living standards, health, and socio-economic stability for beneficiaries.

Key Features of the Scheme

  • Provision of a pucca house or financial aid for construction
  • Houses equipped with basic amenities like water, electricity, and sanitation
  • Focus on women empowerment by registering houses preferably in the name of the woman of the household
  • Transparent selection process for beneficiaries
  • Development of housing colonies with necessary infrastructure
  • Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS) component for home loans

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the Affordable Housing Scheme (Nivas Sahaya), applicants must meet the following criteria:

Residency & Family Status

Must be a resident family of Andhra Pradesh and not own a pucca house anywhere in India

Income Category

Must belong to Economically Weaker Section (EWS - annual income up to ₹3 lakhs) or Low Income Group (LIG - annual income ₹3-₹6 lakhs). Limits may vary.

Woman Headed Household

Preference given to households with an adult female member; house often registered in her name or jointly.

No Prior Benefit

Family should not have availed benefits under any other central or state housing scheme

Land Ownership (for self-construction)

For self-construction aid, must own a plot of land suitable for construction.

Note: Priority is often given to SC/ST, OBC, minorities, differently-abled persons, senior citizens, and other vulnerable groups. Specific project guidelines may have additional criteria.

Benefits

The Affordable Housing Scheme provides the following key benefits to eligible families:

Subsidized Housing Unit

Allotment of a newly constructed house at a highly subsidized price in a developed colony

Financial Aid for Self-Construction

Assistance provided in installments for constructing a house on privately owned land

Credit Linked Subsidy

Interest subsidy on home loans taken for purchase or construction of a house (under CLSS component)

Basic Amenities

Houses provided with essential services like water supply, electricity, and sanitation facilities

Secure Tenure

Ownership rights of the house, preferably in the name of the woman of the household

Improved Living Environment

Access to better community infrastructure and social amenities in planned layouts

Required Documents

The following documents are typically required to apply for the Affordable Housing Scheme:

Identity Proof

Aadhaar Card of all adult family members

Address Proof

Voter ID, Ration Card, or other valid proof of current residence

Income Proof

Income certificate from competent authority or self-declaration as per EWS/LIG norms

Bank Account Details

Passbook copy of an active bank account, preferably linked with Aadhaar

Affidavit

Affidavit stating that the applicant or their family does not own a pucca house anywhere in India

Family Photograph

Recent passport-sized photograph of the family

Land Documents (for self-construction)

Proof of ownership of land if applying for self-construction aid

Note: Caste certificate for SC/ST/OBC and disability certificate for PwD may be required for availing priority. Ensure all documents are up-to-date.

Application Process

The application process for Nivas Sahaya is usually conducted when new projects or phases are announced:

1

Scheme Announcement

Government announces new housing projects or phases through notifications and advertisements

2

Application Submission

Eligible families apply online through SmartSahyata/MeeSeva or offline at designated offices with required documents

3

Scrutiny & Verification

Applications are scrutinized, and field verification is conducted to confirm eligibility

4

Beneficiary Selection

A list of eligible beneficiaries is prepared, often using a lottery system or priority criteria for allotment

5

Allotment / Sanction

Selected beneficiaries receive allotment letters for houses or sanction orders for financial assistance

6

House Construction / Handover

Construction is completed and houses handed over, or financial aid disbursed in stages for self-construction

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the size of the house provided under the scheme?

The size of the house (carpet area) can vary depending on the specific project and location (urban/rural), but it generally adheres to the guidelines set for EWS/LIG housing, often around 30-60 square meters.

Is there any beneficiary contribution required?

Yes, there is usually a nominal beneficiary contribution, which is significantly lower than the market price of the house. For CLSS, the beneficiary takes a loan and receives an interest subsidy.

Can I apply if I own agricultural land but no house?

Owning agricultural land might not disqualify you, provided you do not own a pucca house. However, this depends on the specific scheme guidelines, which should be checked carefully.

How is the selection of beneficiaries done?

After verification of eligibility, beneficiaries are often selected through a transparent process which may involve a computerized lottery system, especially if the number of applicants exceeds available units. Priority categories are also considered.

Can I sell the house allotted under the scheme?

There is usually a lock-in period (e.g., 5-10 years) during which the allotted house cannot be sold or transferred to prevent misuse of the scheme. Specific conditions apply.
